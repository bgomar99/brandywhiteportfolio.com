For this Assignment I was tasked with creating a retro style poster for outer space. I chose to go with cooler tones and utilize the reflections of the Astronauts helmet. I have to rework the astronaut's size several times as well as work on texture for the background to make the whole poster look more retro.
I was tasked with creating the landscape from a book poster and I chose one of my favorite books, Uglies by Scott Westerfeld. I wanted to have more of a framed look with the city as a background.Getting the framing to fit and still show the silhouette of the trees. Adding tiny details helped to distinguish all the different elements.
In my typography class I was tasked with creating a silhouette of an object using just words. There were only supposed to be a total of three colors so I made sure to use it sparingly and make sure that they would stand out. I used a yellow happy on the head to show as a happy person and a red compassionate to show a compassionate heart. Each would have a different size and different fonts with the most important ones being the largest to attract the eyes.
My Cooper Black poster uses only different sizes and forms of the Font Cooper Black. I really like to use focal points on my poster and the big CB with the color slash in the middle. The biggest challenge for this was using just black and white along with using every character available. I tried to make the poster as easy flowing as possible without making it look cramped.
- In this project I was asked to create an abstract portrait using only the letters from my first name. One of the biggest challenges that I faced with this was I wasn't allowed to change the letters in anything other than the size. I had to figure out how to expand the letters and thickness and make the letters unidentifiable.
In this project I was tasked with creating a self portrait using only letters from the alphabet. This was challenging trying to find the different letters to use that would fit my facial shape correctly. I searched through many different fonts to get the different textures for my hair and sweater.
This is a poster with a view of a future space station called station x. This also features a framed style with the silhouette of two small children looking out. Finding a way to show the features of the children looking shocked as they stare out and see the colony was tricky. Although I knew I didn't want the children to be the primary focus, I had to give them an appropriate reaction to seeing out in space for the first time. I settled on just the silhouette and to make the window the frame.
I created stickers that were inspired by the movie Parasite. This features some of the most iconic scenes in the movie. The original assignment was to use a movie and create different icons and patterns from the movie. From there we were asked to pick 10 of the icons to turn into stickers. I had to take the time to figure out how to add the border and make it easier to print.
For this poster the theme was to create an anthropomorphic animals poster for Safari days at the San Diego Zoo. Using only found images, I had to blend the different images together to make it look like it had been photographed. It was one of the first assignments I had ever created so it was very difficult to learn how to cut, layer and blend so it would look natural. The collar was possibly the hardest part as I had to fix the fur on the monkey's neck to make it look as if the shirt was sitting correctly on him.
TThis poster was the second of a three part series to create anthropomorphic animals for Safari days at the San Diego Zoo. For this design I wanted to create more of a cool guy look, so I decided to go with a cheetah for the animal and give him more of a hipster explorer look. It was very difficult to create a human-like expression for the cheetah so I decided to add some glasses and use his "smile" as the facial expression. If I was to redo the poster I would definitely move the text to make it look like it isn't all crowded at the edge of the poster.
